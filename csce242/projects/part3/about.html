About Oktoberfest

Festival Highlights

Vendors

The Bucks-Mont Oktoberfest features an incredible array of local and regional vendors who bring the authentic German experience to life. Our food vendors offer traditional German cuisine including bratwurst, sauerkraut, pretzels, schnitzel, and a variety of German beers and beverages. Many of these vendors have been participating in our festival for decades, bringing their family recipes and time-honored traditions to each celebration.

In addition to food vendors, we host numerous craft vendors showcasing handmade German goods, traditional clothing, jewelry, and decorative items. Local artisans display their work, from hand-carved wooden items to intricate lacework and pottery. These vendors help create the authentic atmosphere that makes our Oktoberfest special, allowing visitors to take home a piece of German culture and tradition.

Our vendor selection process ensures that only the highest quality, most authentic German products and services are featured at our festival. Each vendor is carefully vetted to maintain the traditional atmosphere and cultural authenticity that our visitors have come to expect. We're proud to support local businesses while providing an authentic German experience for all attendees.

History

The Bucks-Mont Oktoberfest has a rich and storied history that spans nearly nine decades. Founded in 1937 by the Vereinigung Erzgebirge Club, this beloved festival began as a small gathering of German immigrants and their families who wanted to celebrate their heritage and share their culture with the local community. What started as a modest celebration has grown into the longest-running German festival in Bucks and Montgomery Counties.

Over the years, the festival has evolved while maintaining its core mission of preserving German traditions and fostering community spirit. The 88th edition in 2025 represents nearly a century of bringing people together through music, food, and celebration. The festival has weathered wars, economic challenges, and even a global pandemic, always emerging stronger and more determined to continue its mission of cultural celebration.

Today, the Oktoberfest continues to honor its founding principles while embracing new generations of festival-goers. The event has become a cherished tradition for families throughout the region, with many attendees returning year after year to reconnect with friends, enjoy traditional German fare, and participate in the time-honored customs that make this festival so special. The Vereinigung Erzgebirge Club remains committed to preserving these traditions for future generations.
